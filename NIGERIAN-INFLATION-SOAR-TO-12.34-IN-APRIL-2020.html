NIGERIAN INFLATION SOAR TO 12.34% IN APRIL 2020

FROM Cynthia felix, Abuja.

21 May, 2020

The consumer price index which measures inflation increased by 12.34 percent (year-on-year) in April 2020 representing 0.08 percent points higher than the rate recorded in March 2020 at 2.26 percent.

According to the National bureau of statistics report, on month-on-month basis, the headline index increased by 1.02 percent in the period, representing 0.18 percent rate higher than the rate recorded in March 2020 (0.84 percent).

"Increases were recorded in all COICOP divisions that yielded the Headline index.

The corresponding twelve-month year-on-year average percentage change for the urban index is 12.26 percent in April 2020, which is higher than 12.15 percent reported in March 2020, while the corresponding rural inflation rate in April 2020 is 11.20 percent compared to 11.14 percent recorded in March 2020".

According to the report, the composite food index rose by 15.03 percent in April compared to 14.98 percent in March 20

This rise in the food index was caused by increases in prices of Potatoes, Yam and other tubers, Bread and cereals, Fish, Oils and fats, Meat, Fruits and Vegetables.

On month-on-month basis, the food sub-index increased by 1.18 percent in April 2020, up by 0.24 percent points from 0.94 percent recorded in March 2020.

"The average annual rate of change of the Food sub-index for the twelve-month period ending April 2020 over the previous twelve-month average was 14.22 percent, 0.11 percent points from the average annual rate of change recorded in March 2020 (14.11) percent".

Similarly, the Core inflation, which excludes the prices of volatile agricultural produce stood at 9.98 percent in April 2020, increased by 0.25 percent when compared with 9.73 percent recorded in March 2020.

Highest increases were recorded in prices of Bicycles, Passenger transport by road, Passenger transport by sea and inland waterways, Paramedical services, Hospital services, pharmaceutical products, Medical services, Motor cycles and Major household appliances whether electric or not.

"On month-on-month basis, the core sub-index increased by 0.93 percent in April 2020. This was up by 0.13 percent when compared with 0.80 percent recorded in March 2020".
