THE SECURITY STUDENT

Buchi Odianjo

13 Jun, 2019

21.2k Views

This is an article I had posted a few years ago but I’ll love to share it here as well. It’s a real-life experience I believe we can all learn from in our various facets of life.

On Thursday the 25th January 2017, waking up, thanking God and grumbling as well because of the workload one was faced with as a Masters student of the Pan Atlantic University. My day had started, I had assignments and proposals to submit. Anxiety seemed to be creeping in, hoping I could meet deadlines; tensed and confused on where to start. Finally, I decided to get a grip on myself, go to the library and start something.

I walked into the library and saw something I believe spoke to my very being. A member of staff studying, not just any staff but ‘a security guard,’ Yes! I put on my glasses just to be certain I wasn’t going colour blind or perhaps I was confusing uniforms, but I was not.

Engrossed with what he was studying, I quickly ran downstairs, got a camera and captured, after all, “seeing is believing” right? Unknown to him, I stood behind and took some pictures of him. I could see the enthusiasm as he had written loads of notes from the library computer he was using and didn’t even realise I was standing behind him.

The next question may sound silly to some but are these trucks and tankers not too heavy for the bridges? If not, are these bridges frequently checked and maintained? If yes, what is the assurance that these tankers drive carefully as they can to avoid catastrophes like them falling off the bridge?; and for the trucks/tankers that carry containers around, how tightly sealed are they? Are they safe enough for transit?

Then I wondered, ‘this Man should have a family, he obviously has a job, and I’m certain he had other duties to fulfil, and yet, he found time to sit on a computer to study.’ I guess he probably wasn’t given the opportunity I had and yet, he found time to study; and there I was grumbling!

Of course, this got me thinking again, ‘can you give your number to a guy that you just met in a danfo bus? Can you ask for a lady’s number in a danfo bus? Can a relationship start with someone you met in a danfo bus?

Share your thoughts…

Indeed, there is no age or time limit to learning. Never think it is too late to learn because of your age, or your schedule. If you believe, focus your mind and learn. God has given every living being the ability to learn and equal time to do so. What you use it for is what makes a difference.

Moral Lesson

1. Anyone can be who they want to be, no matter the age and workload, if only you set your mind to it.

2. Wherever you find yourself, always take advantage of anything that would add to your knowledge.
