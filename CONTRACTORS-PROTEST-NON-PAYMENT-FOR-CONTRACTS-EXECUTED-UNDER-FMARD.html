CONTRACTORS PROTEST NON-PAYMENT FOR CONTRACTS EXECUTED UNDER FMARD

FROM Cynthia felix, Abuja.

21 May, 2020

Contractors on Wednesday protested non payment for contracts executed for the Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Development.

The contractors who stormed the Ministry headquarters in abuja demanded payment of contracts executed for the Ministry in 2018.

The protesting contractors who were chanting " the Minister must go, the Permanent Secretary must go, pay us our money " rejected all entreaties from security agents who were on duty and also refused to vacate the entrance, insisting that either the Minister or the Permanent Secretary must attend to their demand.

"The pandemic and shutdown of advanced economies could push as many as 60 million people into extreme poverty, erasing much of the recent progress made in poverty alleviation, said David Malpass, President, World Bank Group.

A contractor who identified himself as Comrade Lucky said the contractors were awarded various contracts by the ministry since 2018, and had completed the contracts, but the Ministry refused to pay them.

"We are here to take our destiny in our own hands, because since 2018 we were given the contracts we bidded for, which we also executed. We have been coming here for payment with the certificate of completion issued to us by the same Ministry.

The ministry based on whatever consideration have done selective payments leaving some of us out. We have met with both the Minister and the Permanent Secretary, and nothing has been done. At a point, the Permanent Secretary promised to pay us, but later refused to pay.

Speaking further, Lucky stated that some other contractors have died due to heartache resulting from the non payment of the debt owed them by the Ministry.

The International Finance Corporation (IFC) and Multilateral Investment Guarantee Agency (MIGA) have also fast-tracked support to businesses in developing countries, including trade finance and working capital to maintain private sectors, jobs and livelihoods.

According to them, the bank loan they obtained to execute the various contracts have accumulated heavy service charge, which is already affecting their blood pressure.

"IDA-eligible countries that request forbearance on their official bilateral debt payments will have more financial resources to respond to the COVID-19 pandemic and fund critical, lifesaving emergency responses".

Ada Nwahiri, a contractor explained that she executed two borehole projects in Akwa Ibom state for the Ministry, and had been duely certified by relevant engineers of the Ministry, but payment is yet to be made.

Ada Nwahiri " I executed two borehole for the Ministry in Akwa Ibom since 2018. My own money they are owing me is about N23 million . Since the new Permanent Secretary came, he has been promising to pay us for the past seven months, but has not fulfilled his promise.

" I borrowed money from the bank to execute the contract, and I have pulled up bills to pay, but the Ministry has refused to pay us./p>
