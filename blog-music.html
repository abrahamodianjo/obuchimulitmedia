MUSIC MARKETING IN AFRICA FOR UPCOMING ARTIST

Buchi Odianjo

3 Oct, 2019

15.2k Views

“Music they say is food to the soul.” Not everyone may like music but most people love it. That is why the Music Industry all over the world is one of the most illustrious, striving and successful amongst the other aspects of the Entertainment Sector. However, the challenges to succeed especially for an upcoming music Artist is at large. On today’s O’buchi Multimedia Success series, we would be sharing tips and strategies needed for an Upcoming Music Artist to succeed and penetrate the Entertainment Industry.

Who is an Upcoming Music Artist (UMA)?

An Upcoming Artist is a person who is very passionate about the Art of Music which includes: rhythm and rhymes, melody, lyrics, music mixing and mastering, sound orchestration, and the entirety of sound engineering at large. This passion then drives them to continually develop and grow this interest by practice, social media promotion, performing in shows for free, sharing of audio music via links like Soundcloud, Deezer, iTunes, Tidal, Spotify, Apple Music, Audiomark, Pandora, YouTubeMusic, TuneIn and a lot of others. And visual medium such as YouTube, Instagram, Facebook and so on. But then, these strategies are oftentimes not effective enough and leads to time and capital waste especially in the African environment. Hence, we would be sharing the techniques for music promotion a UMA needs to succeed in Africa and beyond, and also strategies to put in place for effectiveness.

Techniques on what an Upcoming Artist needs to do to Succeed (especially in Africa)

O’buchi Multimedia Limited manages a range of Entertainment Industry personalities such as Actors, Brand Influencers, Dancers, On-Air Personalities, MCs and of course Music Artist/Musicians. Therefore, the tips we would be sharing are not only practical but effective and would be broken down in stages for easy understating and application

Stage 1.

Familiarization with immediate environment and neighbourhood: it is important for you to be confident in yourself and your creative art (music) by advertising yourself. Try to gain a level of popularity around you (your village, small town, family, school, etc.) by running roadside performances, word of mouth advertising, patronising the stores around you and just being nice.

Stage 2.

Printing of Music in CD plates: Yes! This is an archaic method but has proven to be one of the most effective. A CD plate typically contains recordings of audio material such as music and audiobook (Wikipedia). It is used in cars, commercial and domestic environment. Therefore making it easy to distribute or gift.

Stage 3.

Strategic CD Branding: a lot of upcoming Artist make the mistake of running stage 1 & 2 and miss stage 3 hoping it would yield results. You CANNOT print a CD plate and leave it blank, it is vital to imprint the following ON THE CD ITSELF:

a. Your name

b. Your Contact info: number, social media handles, email and record label

c. A clear picture of your face

Notice we put

‘ON THE CD ITSELF’
in capital letters above because some artist may print but ignore the CD and focus on the CD cover. That is wrong and a waste of money because the owner of your CD may choose to dispose of/trash the cover or it could get damaged. But the CDs has a higher tendency of been kept carefully and shared to others by the owner.

Stage 4.

. Strategic Music Distribution: This is the aftermath of stage 1. Now, we understand that as an upcoming Artist it is important for you to have a level of popularity around you. Now you have to Follow Up by sharing your branded CDs to a member of your immediate environment for FREE. Yes! I said it, FREE. As a UMA you must understand the popular saying ‘Nothing is Free even in Free Town’ hence, think of this distribution of Free CDs as a long term investment (something that does not bring immediate money but definitely will bring cash in the long term).

Tips for Strategic Music Distribution

Always distribute your music in locations that you can monitor and can easily get feedback from. These locations must be close to you and the owners/attendants familiar with you. Distribute the processed stage 1 to 4 CD as a gift and visit the distributed location once in a while to make sure they listen to your track or album. Also, seek their feedback as this will enable you to know what they think about your craft thus enabling you as an artist to entertain your fans better.

Locations such as:

1. Gym

2. Supermarket/Grocery stores

3. Barbershops and Female Saloons

4. Buses and motor parks

6. Ice cream and yoghurt stands

7. Tailor shops/Fashion Designer office

And finally, always try to Drop a Tip! Some way somehow, a tip always gains the interest of most humans, especially the gatekeepers or intermediaries such as security guards, salespersons, chauffeurs, butlers, bar attendants, gym instructors, etc. A tip can be in the form of cash, a compliment, a purchase or even a referral.

In summary, the Music Industry is one aspect of the Entertainment Industry that would never go dry in lucrativeness. Only the Upcoming Artist who is passionate and optimistic that applies to the stage 1 to 4 rule and other strategic tips of music distribution would be able to tap into the money-making aspect and fame of the Industry. These strategic, tips and methods can be applied in other sectors: by Brand Influencers, Dancers, On-Air Personalities, MCs, Actors, Brand Influencers and other players in the Entertainment Industry.
